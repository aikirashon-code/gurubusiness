How Remote Online Notarization Works in New Jersey

A complete legal guide to the multi-layered identity proofing, PKI sealing, and institutional acceptance framework under N.J.S.A. 52:7-10 et seq.

Remote Online Notarization (RON) fundamentally reimagines how legal documents get notarized. Instead of requiring all parties to be physically present in the same room, RON uses a live, two-way audio-visual connection alongside advanced identity verification technology to complete a fully legal, court-recognized notarization from any device — phone, tablet, or laptop.

New Jersey officially authorized RON under N.J.S.A. 52:7-10 et seq., establishing the legal infrastructure for platform requirements, identity proofing standards, electronic seal mandates, and long-term journal archival obligations. GuruBusiness operates fully within this framework.

1

The Legal Foundation: N.J.S.A. 52:7-10

The New Jersey Remote Notarization statute authorizes commissioned notaries to perform notarial acts for remotely located individuals using approved communication technology. The law imposes strict obligations on every RON provider:

Real-time AV communication — the notary and signer must be able to see and hear each other simultaneously throughout the entire session with no degradation or interruption.

Identity proofing — the notary must use an identity-proofing service that meets or exceeds NIST Special Publication 800-63-3 Level of Assurance 2 standards before the session begins.

10-year secure retention — the RON provider must maintain encrypted recordings of all sessions and copies of all electronic journals for a minimum of ten years from the date of the notarial act.

2

Multi-Layer Identity Proofing

RON identity verification is layered by design — no single check is sufficient on its own. GuruBusiness deploys a three-layer authentication stack before any session begins:

Layer Stack

A

Credential Analysis (CAV)

The signer photographs their government-issued ID. AI-powered systems scan for microprinting, holograms, UV overlays, and dimensional accuracy to detect counterfeit credentials with near-zero false positives.

B

Knowledge-Based Authentication (KBA)

The system generates five dynamic, out-of-wallet multiple-choice questions derived from the signer's personal credit and public record history — questions only the real person should be able to answer within 2 minutes.

C

Live Biometric Liveness Check

A brief facial scan confirms the live person matches the credential photograph — preventing the use of printed photos or deepfake videos to spoof the system during the AV session.

What if KBA Fails?

Up to 20% of legitimate users fail KBA on the first attempt due to thin credit files, frozen credit, or recent moves. This is normal and not a red flag. If this happens, our system routes you seamlessly to a mobile notary booking — no data is lost, no extra charge applies.

3

PKI Sealing & Document Integrity

Once the signing session is complete, the notary affixes their electronic seal using Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) cryptography. This process:

Creates a unique cryptographic hash of the final document — any future alteration, even a single character, produces a completely different hash, permanently invalidating the certificate and alerting every verifying party.

Embeds the notary's digital certificate — issued by a NIST-approved Certificate Authority — directly into the PDF metadata, visible to any PDF reader that validates signatures.

Produces a time-stamped, audit-logged chain-of-custody trail — recording every document access event, the platform session ID, KBA pass confirmation, and the notary's commission expiration date in a tamper-evident ledger.
